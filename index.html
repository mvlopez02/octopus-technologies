Empowering Energy Management: Innovate, Measure, Optimize

Welcome!

Octopus Technologies, a team of students from the Electronic Engineering program at Universidad del Norte, is immersed in an exciting project focused on building a Power Meter for a Mobile Platform's Battery Bank. This challenge is divided into four well-defined stages: creating an OpAmp from scratch, integrating it into the power measurement system, testing and adjustments, and finally presenting the circuits on a PCB packaged in the final product.

Stage 1

This stage focuses on the research and foundational work for the project. It includes reviewing the state of the art and developing the theoretical framework, establishing the methodology, and creating a detailed timeline (WBS). Specifications are gathered, and the functional breakdown of the system is performed. Conceptual simulations, block diagrams, and flowcharts are also created to define the initial structure.

Stage 2

The second stage involves the design and construction of a custom operational amplifier (Op-Amp). This includes selecting topologies and technologies, conducting detailed simulations, and assembling the Op-Amp. Once built, functionality tests are conducted, and specifications are generated based on the results.

Stage 3

In this stage, the custom Op-Amp is integrated into the system's block diagram. Detailed simulations and iterative redesigns are carried out, followed by preliminary setups and adjustments. The designs are refined, and a risk assessment (including Failure Mode and Effects Analysis, FMEA) is conducted to ensure robustness.

Stage 4

The final stage defines a comprehensive experimental plan and tests the system’s functionality. Additional elements, such as Arduino integration or visualizations, may be included. The project is then prepared for final delivery, including assembling cases, creating labels, and completing documentation for the final handover.
